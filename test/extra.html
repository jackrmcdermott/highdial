Today, upward mobility has become a coin toss.

For decades, education has been a sure path to stable, well-paying careers. In the 1940s, 90% of students could expect to out-earn their parents.

Today, a student has only a 50% chance of realizing a career that will enable them to out-earn their parents—and low-income, first-generation, and minority students have an even steeper climb to maximize their career earnings potential.¹
