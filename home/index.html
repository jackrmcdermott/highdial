Why do we need new career pathways?

⌄

Today, upward mobility has become a coin toss.

For decades, education has been a sure path to stable, well-paying careers. In the 1940s, 90% of students could expect to out-earn their parents.

Today, students have only a 50% chance of realizing a career that will enable them to out-earn their parents—and low-income, first-generation, and minority students have an even steeper climb to maximize their career earnings potential.¹

Most workers will enter their career without a
4-year college degree.

As technological change has disrupted industries, schools have struggled to keep pace in preparing skilled workers. 3+ million students now enter the workforce without a four-year college degree each year.

Most young adults will pursue a college degree, but 45% who start don’t finish. Along the way, students have amassed $1.5 trillion in student loan debt. Even those who do graduate start their career with $30,000 in debt on average.²
