Why now?

⌄

Today, upward mobility is a coin toss.

For decades, higher education was a sure path to stable, well-paying careers. In the 1940s, 90% of students could expect to out-earn their parents.

Students now have only a 50% chance of out-earning their parents—and low-income, first-gen, and minority students have an even steeper climb to reach their career potential.1

Most students will start their career without a 4-year college degree.

Most young adults will pursue college after high school, but only 55% will graduate within six years.

Young adults now start their careers with an average of $30,000 in student loan debt.23

Career exposure & meaningful work can give students a leg-up

Students can find enriching work experiences, save for college, and grow career-ready skills starting today.

Millions of high-quality roles go unfilled; employers struggle to find qualified, motivated candidates, and are increasingly offering tuition assistance programs.6

That's why we’re building Highdial.

We believe there’s a better way to connect students to meaningful employment during and after high school.
